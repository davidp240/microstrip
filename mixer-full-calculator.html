RF Mixing & Heterodyning Analysis

This mathematical framework provides the physical models for frequency mixing, intermodulation distortion, and network parameters. Highly critical for engineering transceivers, downconverters, and passive diplexing filters.

CH.01 // Time-Domain Carriers ANALYSIS

Mathematical modeling of ideal carrier frequencies applied to the system ports.

$$v_{RF}(t) = A_{RF}\cos(\omega_{RF}t + \phi_{RF})$$
$$v_{LO}(t) = A_{LO}\cos(\omega_{LO}t)$$

Parameters:

A_RF / A_LO : Peak Volts ω_RF / ω_LO : Ang. Freq (rad/s)
CH.02 // Product Modulation THEORY

Ideal multiplication of RF and Local Oscillator carriers yields the upper and lower sidebands.

$$\cos(A)\cos(B) = \frac{1}{2}\left[\cos(A-B) + \cos(A+B)\right]$$
$$v_{IF}(t) = \frac{A_{RF}A_{LO}}{2}\cos((\omega_{RF}-\omega_{LO})t)$$

The factor of $\frac{1}{2}$ represents an inherent 6 dB loss of signal power in passive double-sided converters.

CH.03 // Linearity and IIP3 CRITICAL

Calculation of the Third-Order Intercept Point (IIP3) indicating power saturation levels.

$$\text{IIP3} = P_{IN} + \frac{\Delta}{2} \quad [\text{dBm}]$$
$$P_{1\text{dB}} \approx \text{IIP3} - 9.6 \quad [\text{dBm}]$$

$\Delta$ is the relative difference in dB between the fundamental signal power and the third-order intermodulation sideband ($2f_1 - f_2$).

CH.04 // Noise and Cascades (Friis) THEORY

The Friis formula computes noise factors ($F$) across cascaded receiver blocks.

$$F_{sys} = F_1 + \frac{F_2 - 1}{G_1} + \frac{F_3 - 1}{G_1 G_2}$$
$$T_e = (F - 1) \cdot 290 \quad [\text{K}]$$

Note that high gain ($G_1$) in the primary low-noise amplifier (LNA) reduces the noise contribution of subsequent stages (such as the mixer).
